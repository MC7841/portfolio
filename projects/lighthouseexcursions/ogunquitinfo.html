Ogunquit Info


Geography

Ogunquit rests right on the coast by the sea and, it is a part of Southern Portland. The town has 3.5 miles of beaches and coastlines. The name in Abenaki language means “A beautiful place by the sea”.

History

Originally Ogunquit was a small fishing village known as Fish Cove back in the late 1800s. The town was unprotected from the harsh Atlantic Storms and had to haul their boats inland each night. The people formed an association to dig a channel to connect Fish Cove with the Josias River. When the channel was complete, they had connected the town to the mainland with a bridge. This connection led to artists traveling to live in the village. Their influence transformed the town into a popular art colony and tourist destination.

Things To Do

Ogunquit offers a variety of activities such as deep-sea fishing, whale watching, sailing, and sea kayaking.

Ogunquit Museum of Art: This small museum is dedicated to preserving the town’s artistic heritage and origins. They have a permanent collection of American Art from the 1800s to the present. They also host various classes and lectures for the community to promote the influence of art.

Marginal Way: A historical trail that runs along the coastline from Perkins Cove to Ogunquit Beach. Relax and catch beautiful views of the coastline. This trail is a must-see destination.
